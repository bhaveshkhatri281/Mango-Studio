HPP Technology Explained: How We Kill Bacteria Without Heat

High Pressure Processing uses cold water at 600MPa of pressure. The science is fascinating and the results speak for themselves.

April 28, 2026·6 min read·By Mango Studio Team
M

For decades, the beverage industry faced an impossible choice: pasteurize the juice to make it safe and shelf-stable, thereby destroying its nutritional value and flavor, or serve it raw, risking bacterial contamination and facing a shelf life measured in mere days. High Pressure Processing (HPP) is the technological breakthrough that finally resolved this paradox.

The Science of Pressure

Also known as Pascalization or cold pasteurization, HPP doesn't rely on thermal energy to eliminate pathogens. Instead, it uses immense hydrostatic pressure. Once our mango juice is bottled and sealed, the bottles are loaded into a specialized steel vessel. This vessel is flooded with cold water, and massive pumps pressurize the water to incredible levels—typically 600 Megapascals (MPa), or roughly 87,000 pounds per square inch (psi).

"To put 600MPa into perspective, it's equivalent to the pressure found at the bottom of the Marianas Trench—the deepest point in the world's oceans—multiplied by six."

Cellular Disruption Without Chemical Change

When bacteria like E. coli, Salmonella, or Listeria are subjected to this level of pressure, their cellular structures fail. The pressure disrupts their cell membranes and denatures the specific proteins they need to survive, effectively neutralizing them. However—and this is the crucial part—the pressure does not affect covalent bonds.

Vitamins, antioxidants, enzymes, and flavor molecules are held together by strong covalent bonds. Because these bonds remain unaffected by hydrostatic pressure, the nutritional profile and the delicate, complex flavor of the raw mango are perfectly preserved.

Why HPP is the Future of Food

The benefits of this process extend beyond just safety and nutrition:

  • Clean Labels: Because HPP neutralizes spoilage organisms, there is no need to add artificial preservatives, chemical additives, or excessive sugar to extend shelf life.
  • True Flavor: Heat fundamentally changes the chemistry of food. HPP ensures the juice tastes exactly as it did the moment it was pressed.
  • Sustainability: While HPP equipment is expensive, the process uses recycled water and relatively low amounts of electricity compared to continuous thermal processing plants.

At Mango Studio, our partnership with a Swiss-engineered HPP facility allows us to deliver a product that is 100% safe, boasts a 45-day refrigerated shelf life, and retains every ounce of nutritional value provided by the Ratnagiri Alphonso mango.
